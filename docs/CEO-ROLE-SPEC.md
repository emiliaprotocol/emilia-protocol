# Chief Executive Officer — EMILIA Protocol

## Mission

Lead EMILIA Protocol from a strong technical protocol into a trusted institution and category-defining company.

The CTO and technical co-founder remains the architectural and protocol lead. The CEO owns the company-building side: fundraising, partnerships, standards engagement, ecosystem strategy, go-to-market, team building, and institutional credibility.

## About EP

EMILIA Protocol (EP) is a protocol for trust evaluation and appeals across counterparties, software, and machine actors. EP sits alongside the emerging agent stack: MCP for tool access, A2A for coordination, ACP/UCP/AP2 for commerce and payments, EP for trust evaluation and appeals.

## Why this role exists

The protocol has reached the point where technical vision is no longer the main bottleneck. The next phase requires real pilots, host/platform integrations, standards positioning, fundraising, governance credibility, and partnerships with marketplaces, registries, enterprises, and ecosystem players.

## What the CEO owns

**1. Fundraising** — craft the financing story, run investor process, raise seed/Series A, position EP as protocol + infrastructure + trust network

**2. Partnerships** — secure launch partners across commerce, software, and marketplaces. Build relationships with MCP ecosystem, GitHub, commerce platforms, marketplaces, enterprise buyers. Turn pilots into proof.

**3. Standards and governance** — represent EP with AAIF, NIST, and related standards conversations. Shape governance positioning. Recruit outside advisors and ecosystem allies.

**4. Go-to-market** — define initial wedge and expansion path. Shape messaging: not a score, not just commerce — trust evaluation and appeals for machine-mediated systems.

**5. Company building** — hire the commercial and operational team around the CTO. Create execution cadence. Build a company that can support protocol, product, and ecosystem growth.

## What the CTO owns

Architecture, evaluator/writer/core system design, protocol evolution, conformance, technical roadmap, implementation rigor. This role is not a replacement for the technical founder — it is the complementary company-building counterpart.

## Ideal candidate

**Must have:** experience fundraising for technical infrastructure or protocol products, strong partnership instincts, ability to sell a category before it is obvious, comfort with standards bodies and ecosystem diplomacy, ability to work with a deeply technical founder without flattening the vision.

**Preferred background:** developer infrastructure, marketplaces, commerce infrastructure, trust & safety, security/identity/risk, protocol/ecosystem businesses.

**Not a fit:** someone looking to turn EP into just another SaaS tool, uncomfortable with open standards/protocol strategy, or wants to replace the technical founder.

## First 12 months

**0–3 months:** sharpen financing narrative, identify pilot targets, structure governance/advisory path

**3–6 months:** close first serious pilots, secure ecosystem relationships, raise capital

**6–12 months:** turn pilots into reference deployments, create standards visibility, build first commercial operating layer

## Compensation

Open to founder-level equity, CEO title, board-level role, strong alignment with long-term protocol mission.

## Core trait

The ideal CEO believes that trust will become one of the most important infrastructure layers in machine-mediated systems — and knows how to build the institution around that idea.
