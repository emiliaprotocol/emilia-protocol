The problem

Fifty years of security asked "who is allowed in?"

The dominant users of software are no longer humans — they're AI agents that move money, change records, and delete data on the fly. The unanswered question is no longer authentication. It's authorization at the moment of action:

Who approved that exact action — before it executed?

Why now

"A human was in the loop" is unfalsifiable.

  • CISOs are blocking agent deployment — sitting on AI budget they can't spend because compliance can't answer "who authorized that?"
  • Regulators now require human oversight (EU AI Act Art. 14) — but no one can verify it after the fact.
  • Decision logs are testimony — what the operator says happened, in a database the operator controls.
02 · Why nowEMILIA Protocol

What EP is

Authorization receipts.

Before a high-risk action runs, a named human approves the exact action on their own device (WebAuthn / Face ID). EP emits a signed evidence packet anyone can verify offline, with no account, without trusting our servers.

Alter one byte and verification fails. The receipt stays valid even if EMILIA disappears. npx @emilia-protocol/crash-test

Wedge — a market where the two-person rule is already law

Healthcare.

EP's two-person rule is already mandated practice: the ISMP / Joint Commission independent double-check for high-alert medications. EP turns that attestation into tamper-evident, offline-verifiable evidence — PHI-free by construction.

Same primitive covers capital procurement (kills payment-redirect fraud) and answers Article 14 for medical AI. Go-to-market: healthcare-AI vendors + procurement platforms embed EP (B2B2H), not direct hospital sales.
