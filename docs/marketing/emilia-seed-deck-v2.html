01 · Why this, why nowEMILIA Protocol
The gap

"A human approved this"
is a claim no one can check.

Today the proof lives in the operator's own editable logs — forgeable, backfillable, un-auditable by a regulator, court, or insurer without trusting the very party under review. Logs tell you what happened. They cannot prove who authorized it. That evidence gap is a category.

03 · What EP isEd25519 · RFC 8785 · offline · one-time
The product · the category

EMILIA Gate —
the Consequence Firewall.

Deny-by-default at the actuator boundary. A consequential action runs only with a valid, in-scope, sufficiently-assured, non-replayed receipt — then emits proof it ran. A new security category: not antivirus (is this file bad?), not a firewall (is this packet allowed?) — is this machine allowed to take this consequence, and can you prove who authorized it?

Why now

Regulation is converging on
the artifact we already ship.

EU AI Act Art. 14/12 (human oversight + tamper-evident logs), NIST AI RMF, ISO 42001, eIDAS 2.0 qualified ledgers, FERC 2222 / NERC (energy), DoD 3000.09 (defense) — all demand verifiable human authorization and specify no format for it. EP is that format. There is a ~24-month window before a reference standard locks in. Whoever is the running, open, formally-verified standard when the rules land, wins.
