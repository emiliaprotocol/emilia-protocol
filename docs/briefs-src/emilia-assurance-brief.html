EMILIA for Audit & Assurance

A testable control for agentic AI: pre-execution human authorization you can re-perform from raw evidence, without trusting the operator or the AI vendor.

01The audit gap

AI agents now execute inside financially-relevant processes — releasing payments, changing vendor master records, posting adjustments, deploying to production. When an engagement asks who authorized this action, the record most organizations can produce is the AI vendor's own activity log. That is a record produced by the party whose product did the harm: it is testimony, not evidence. It cannot be recomputed by anyone else's code, and its integrity rests entirely on the operator being both honest and uncompromised.

Post-hoc logs also arrive after the money has moved. A control an auditor can test needs to bind a named human to the exact action before execution, and the resulting record needs to verify independently of the system that produced it.

02The control, in one sentence

A named human's device-bound signature over the exact action, captured before execution, verifiable offline by anyone's code.

Enforced by EMILIA Gate, a deny-by-default policy-enforcement point: a guarded action runs only with a receipt that is valid (Ed25519 over canonical JSON, pinned issuer), in-scope (bound to the exact action fields), sufficiently assured (cryptographically verified tier — WebAuthn device signoff or M-of-N quorum, never a self-asserted field), fresh, and unused (one-time consumption). Every decision, allow or deny, is appended to a hash-chained, tamper-evident evidence log.

04Re-performance — trust nothing, recompute everything

The stack is built for re-performance, not inspection. The auditor's own code recomputes every claim from the raw artifacts: each Ed25519 signature against pinned issuer keys, the hash chain over canonical JSON, the credited assurance tier from per-signer proofs. Nothing the operator asserts is taken on faith. One command replays a full agentic action trail and re-verifies it end to end:

$ node examples/audit/agentic-audit-trail-demo.mjs

Runs offline against the repository's reference implementation. Any altered or removed record breaks the chain and the verification reports exactly where.

05What sets the control apart

/ The cryptographic two-person rule

For hard actions, the required tier is quorum: M-of-N distinct humans with distinct keys, threshold met, each signer's assertion bound to the exact action and time window — and each verified independently. "Two people approved this" stops being a screenshot of an approval UI and becomes an artifact the audit can recompute.

/ Break-glass as evidence, never a bypass

There is no "disable the gate" switch. An emergency override is itself a signed M-of-N grant — scoped to listed action types, time-bounded, attributed (reason and incident reference are required), single-use, and appended to the same evidence chain. The exception path produces stronger evidence, not a gap.

Module contract: NO EVIDENCE ENTRY, NO OVERRIDE.

Status & boundaries
  • Open standard, individual drafts. EMILIA Protocol is an open, Apache-2.0 specification. Its Internet-Drafts are active on the IETF's individual stream: drafts by their authors, not IETF-endorsed and not adopted standards.
  • Reference verifiers. Verifiers exist in JavaScript, Python, and Go, maintained in one repository. Cross-language agreement is a consistency check, not full independence; an independent clean-room reimplementation is underway.
  • What verification proves. Signature validity, action binding, tier crediting, and log integrity — not the business correctness of an authorized action. Principal identities are as pinned by the deployer, not independently verified.
  • Not an attestation. EMILIA Protocol is not an auditor. This brief and the artifacts it describes exist to support an audit; nothing here is an attestation, examination, or opinion, and none of these documents makes any system "compliant out of the box."
