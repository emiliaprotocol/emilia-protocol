EU AI Act · Article 14 · Annex III timeline: 2 December 2027

Proof, not measurement.

A score describes an oversight process. A receipt establishes a narrower authorization fact.

The assessment question

Article 14 addresses effective human oversight, including the ability to disregard, override, reverse, intervene, or stop where appropriate. EMILIA does not provide the whole oversight program. It gives one configured control a durable answer to this narrower question:

Did a relying-party-pinned approver key authorize this exact consequential action, under the verified context, before the executor acted?

What the evidence can establish

Signature and exact-action binding; pinned approver and policy inputs; WebAuthn RP ID, user-presence and user-verification flags in Class-A mode; distinct-signoff quorum where required; one-time consumption at the executor; tamper-evident retained decision records.

What must remain separate

Legal identity proofing, competence, comprehension, freedom from coercion, automation-bias controls, safe-state design, action correctness, complete routing through the Gate, retention adequacy, and the overall Article 14 assessment.
