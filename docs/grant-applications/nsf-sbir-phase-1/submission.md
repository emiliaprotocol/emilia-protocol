# NSF SBIR Phase I — Submission Instructions

**Program**: NSF America's Seed Fund SBIR / STTR
**Solicitation**: NSF 26-510 (program relaunched June 2, 2026 after a freeze)
**Primary topic**: AI — Artificial Intelligence / AI7 Technologies for Trustworthy AI
**Secondary fit**: CA — Cybersecurity and Authentication (CA8, CA3, CA2)
**URL**: https://seedfund.nsf.gov/
**Cost**: Free (Project Pitch)
**Award**: Phase I — up to $305,000 / 6–18 months
**Format**: Project Pitch (web form) → email invitation from NSF → full Phase I proposal

> Verified June 2026 against the NSF 26-510 solicitation and seedfund.nsf.gov.
> Note the **topic-code change**: the old draft used "CT — Cybersecurity &
> Authentication." The current taxonomy has no "CT." Cybersecurity is now
> **CA** (CA1–CA12), and there is a distinct **AI** topic (AI1–AI8) whose
> AI7 — Technologies for Trustworthy AI is the better home for this project.

## Step 1 — Project Pitch (do this first)

1. Go to https://seedfund.nsf.gov/ and start a Project Pitch via the MyWork
   Community portal. Create an account if needed.
2. Select **Submit a Project Pitch**.
3. Choose Topic: **AI — Artificial Intelligence**, subtopic **AI7 Technologies
   for Trustworthy AI** (primary recommendation). CA — Cybersecurity and
   Authentication is the secondary option; confirm fit with a program officer
   first (see "What I'd send before submitting").
4. Paste each section of `application.md` into the matching form field:
   - "Briefly Describe the Technology Innovation" → section 1 (≤500 words)
   - "Technical Objectives and Challenges" → section 2 (≤500 words)
   - "Market Opportunity" → section 3 (≤250 words)
   - "Company and Team" → section 4 (≤250 words)
5. Submit.

NSF program staff respond by email. Either:
- **Invited**: you may submit a full Phase I proposal. The invitation is valid
  for the **next two full-proposal deadlines**.
- **Not invited**: feedback provided. You may resubmit a revised pitch.

**Pitch limits (NSF 26-510):** maximum **2 Project Pitch submissions per
company per 12-month period**; no more than 3 submissions for the same
project/technology. Pitches are accepted on a rolling basis (no fixed pitch
deadline) — but the *full proposal* has fixed deadlines, so submit the pitch
with enough lead time to be invited before the deadline you're targeting.

## Step 2 — Full Phase I (only if invited)

If invited, the full Phase I proposal is submitted in Research.gov. It includes
a Project Description (technical), a **Commercialization Plan (the section
reviewers weigh most)**, PI biosketch and key personnel, budget justification,
letters of support from prospective customers, and facilities/equipment.

For the Commercialization Plan, the strongest evidence would be 1–3 named
county finance offices that have agreed to a GovGuard observe-mode pilot
(paid pilot or design-partner LOI). As of now these are in outreach, not
signed — converting at least one before the full proposal materially helps.

## Full-proposal deadlines (NSF 26-510)

- **July 27, 2026** (first deadline of the relaunched program)
- **November 4, 2026**, then the first Wednesday of November annually
- **March 4, 2027**, then the first Thursday of March annually
- **July 7, 2027**, then the first Wednesday of July annually

To make the **July 27, 2026** full-proposal deadline you would need a Project
Pitch invitation in hand well before it. If that's too tight, target
**November 4, 2026** and submit the pitch soon to leave room for the email
turnaround and (if needed) one revision.

## Eligibility checklist

- [x] Small business **more than 50% owned by a US citizen** (the founder);
      not majority VC/PE/hedge-fund owned.
- [x] Fewer than 500 employees (including affiliates).
- [x] Organized for profit, place of business in the US, R&D majority performed
      in the US.
- [ ] **PI primary-employment requirement — READ THIS.** NSF requires the PI's
      *primary* employment to be with the small business **at the time of
      award and for the award's duration** — defined as **at least 51%
      employed by the small business**, with outside employment of more than
      **19.6 hours/week** considered in conflict. **Crucially, this is required
      at the time of award, NOT at the time of submission.** The founder may
      submit the Project Pitch and the full proposal while still primarily
      employed elsewhere. But if NSF makes the award, the founder must reduce
      outside employment to ≤19.6 hrs/week (or name a different qualifying PI)
      to accept it. **Decide the plan for this before accepting an award —
      it does not block the pitch or the proposal.**
- [ ] **Registrations** (needed for the full proposal, not the pitch):
      SAM.gov (System for Award Management), an NSF ID / Research.gov account,
      and SBA Company Registry. SAM can take 1–2+ weeks the first time — start
      it in parallel with the Project Pitch.

## Topic-fit / program-officer check before submitting

A short, well-formed email to a program officer often gets your pitch onto the
right desk and confirms the topic before a 3-week round trip. Suggested note:

> "Hi [Officer Name] — I'm preparing an NSF SBIR Project Pitch under
> AI7 (Technologies for Trustworthy AI) for an open-standard, formally-verified
> authorization-receipt layer for AI-agent actions (EMILIA Protocol):
> tamper-evident, offline-verifiable cryptographic evidence of who authorized an
> agent's irreversible action, under what policy. IETF Internet-Draft posted,
> 26 TLA+ properties model-checked, three-language verifiers and a conformance
> suite published, Apache 2.0. The Phase I research targets escalation-policy
> synthesis and provable multi-operator federation. Would AI7 or CA
> (Cybersecurity & Authentication) be the better home? — Iman"

## Honesty guardrails (keep in every version)

- No customers — pilots are in active outreach only.
- "Irreversible," not "consequential."
- "The receipt proves," never "EMILIA proves."
- The live two-operator federation is disclosed as both-EMILIA-operated.
