# NSF SBIR Phase I — Submission Instructions

**Program**: NSF America's Seed Fund SBIR / STTR
**Topic**: CT — Cybersecurity & Authentication
**URL**: https://seedfund.nsf.gov/applications/
**Cost**: Free
**Award**: Phase I — $305,000 / 12 months
**Format**: Project Pitch (web form, ~3 pages) → invitation → full Phase I proposal (~50 pages)

## Step 1 — Project Pitch (do this first)

1. Go to https://seedfund.nsf.gov/applications/
2. Sign in / create an account using `iman@emiliaprotocol.ai`
3. Select **Submit a Project Pitch**
4. Choose Topic: **CT — Cybersecurity & Authentication**
5. Paste each section of `application.md` into the matching form field:
   - "Briefly Describe the Technology Innovation" → section 1
   - "Technical Objectives and Challenges" → section 2
   - "Market Opportunity" → section 3
   - "Company and Team" → section 4
6. Submit.

NSF returns one of two responses within ~3 weeks:
- **Encouraged**: invitation to submit full Phase I proposal (~30-day window).
- **Not encouraged**: feedback on what to address. You can resubmit a revised pitch.

## Step 2 — Full Phase I (only if invited)

If invited, the Phase I proposal is ~50 pages and includes:
- Project Description (15 pages technical)
- Commercialization Plan (5 pages — VERY important to NSF)
- Personnel section (PI biosketch, key personnel)
- Budget Justification
- Letters of Support (from prospective customers — try to get 1–3)
- Facilities & Equipment

Reuse `docs/AWS-GRANT-APPLICATION.md` heavily for technical content.
The Commercialization Plan is the section reviewers weigh most — make
sure it includes 2–3 named prospective customers with the type of
engagement (paid pilot, design partner, etc.) confirmed by email or LOI.

## Eligibility checklist

- [x] US-owned small business (>50% US ownership)
- [x] Fewer than 500 employees
- [x] PI > 50% time-based in the US
- [x] Project performs majority of R&D in the US
- [ ] **Pre-submission registration**: register at https://www.sam.gov/
  (System for Award Management) AND https://research.gov before submitting.
  This takes 1–2 weeks the first time. Do this in parallel with the Project
  Pitch — Pitch can be submitted before SAM registration is complete, but
  full Phase I cannot.

## Cycle / deadline notes

The NSF SBIR Phase I has rolling submissions across multiple windows per
year. Project Pitches accept continuously; full Phase I has fixed
deadlines (typically March, June, October). After your Pitch is
encouraged, NSF gives you ~30 days to submit the full proposal.

## Contact

NSF SBIR program officers for Topic CT are listed at
https://seedfund.nsf.gov/topics/ — you can email questions before
submitting. Doing so (a short, well-formed question) often gets your
Project Pitch onto a specific officer's desk and accelerates review.

## What I'd send before submitting

A short LinkedIn / email outreach to 3 program officers:
> "Hi [Officer Name] — I'm finalizing an NSF SBIR Project Pitch under
> Topic CT for an open-standard, formally-verified pre-action
> authorization protocol for AI agent systems (EMILIA Protocol).
> 26 TLA+ theorems verified, Apache 2.0, runtime in production. Before
> I submit, would you be open to a 5-minute fit check on whether
> CT or a different topic is the better home? — Iman"

NSF officers respond to these. Knowing the right topic before you submit
saves a 3-week round trip if the fit is off.
