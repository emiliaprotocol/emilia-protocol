# Emergent Ventures — How to Submit

**Program:** Emergent Ventures, Mercatus Center at George Mason University (apply under the **EV AI** vertical).
**Where:** https://www.mercatus.org/emergent-ventures → click **Apply Now**. In the application, indicate the bet is AI-related so it routes to the **EV AI** vertical (Mercatus runs a dedicated AI track).
**Eligibility:** Open to applicants **13+**, anywhere in the world. No institutional affiliation required — solo founders are squarely in scope. EV explicitly funds individuals and early, unconventional projects.
**Time to apply:** ~30 minutes. The form is short and unstructured; there is no rigid template, no budget spreadsheet, no letters of reference required.
**Contact:** EV does not run a heavy correspondence process; general program contact is **emergentventures@mercatus.org**. Tyler Cowen (program lead) reviews; decisions are fast (weeks, sometimes faster) and communicated by email.

## Notes
- **Keep it tight and bold.** EV rewards ambition and clarity over completeness; do not pad. The "two-person rule for AI agents" hook should appear in the first sentence.
- **Lead with shipped proof.** EV backs people who do, not propose. Foreground EP-QUORUM-v1 being built + tri-language-verified and the live demo.
- **State the ask range, not a hard number,** unless the form forces one — EV grants are flexible and they size to the person.
- **Do not re-pitch** venues already submitted (Manifund, LTFF, OpenAI Cybersecurity, Anthropic Research, NSF SBIR, NIST AISIC). EV is independent of all of them; no conflict, no need to mention unless asked about other funding.
- After submitting, no follow-up is expected unless EV reaches out. Log status in `docs/grant-applications/README.md`.
