# EMILIA Protocol — Realistic 90-Day Plan

**Date:** 2026-04-28
**Replaces:** the 14-day plan in §10 of the monetization audit
**Owner:** Iman Schrock

---

## Why this exists

The external audit's §10 "14-Day Execution Plan" set targets that ignore
the actual sales cycle EMILIA is selling into:

> Goal: 5 meetings, 2 pilot proposals, 1 paid pilot — in 14 days.

Government cold-email reply rates to unit-level addresses are 2–5%, not
the 25% that 5 meetings from 20 sends would require. State procurement
cycles for even a $25K POC are 60–180 days. Hitting "1 paid pilot in 14
days" requires either a warm intro (we have none) or selling something
other than government (we're not).

The 14-day plan was morale theater. This is what's actually achievable.

---

## Honest baselines

| Metric | Realistic baseline |
|---|---|
| Cold-email reply rate (state agency unit-level) | **2–5%** |
| Reply → first-call conversion | 30–50% |
| First-call → "interested in pilot" | 10–20% |
| "Interested in pilot" → paid POC contract | 30–50% |
| Time from first reply to signed POC | **60–180 days** |
| Time from signed POC to first invoice | 30–90 days additional |

Math from 20 high-quality cold emails:
- Replies: **0–1**, expected 0.6
- First calls: **0–1**, expected 0.2
- Paid POC contracts in 90 days: **0**, expected 0.0–0.1

To realistically land 1 paid POC in 90 days, the actual outreach volume
needed is ~150 emails — across a sustained cadence with follow-ups,
references, and at least one warm-intro path opened.

## 90-day goals (achievable)

| Day range | Goal | Success metric |
|---|---|---|
| Day 1–7   | Tier-1 outreach landed | 20 sends, 0–2 replies, 1 follow-up sequence underway |
| Day 8–21  | Tier-2 expansion + first calls booked | 30 additional sends, 1–2 first calls scheduled |
| Day 22–45 | First pilot scoping conversations | 1–2 30-min calls, 1 follow-up technical demo |
| Day 46–75 | Pilot proposal + LOI | 1 written pilot proposal, 1 LOI under negotiation |
| Day 76–90 | First paid POC OR confirmed pipeline | 1 of: signed POC contract / FY27 budget allocation confirmed / advance to procurement |

**Net realistic 90-day outcome:** **0 signed contracts, 1 active deal in
late-stage scoping, 3–5 warm conversations, 1 confirmed FY27 budget
slot.** That is success, not failure. Anyone telling you 1 signed
contract in 90 days from cold government outreach is either selling you
a fantasy or has a warm intro.

## What faster looks like (warm-intro paths)

If the goal is "first paid revenue in 90 days," cold government is the
wrong wedge. Faster paths, in priority order:

1. **AAIF working-group → member referral.** AAIF members include
   regulated-industry CISOs. A single warm intro from AAIF leadership
   can compress the sales cycle 3–6 months.

2. **NIST AI safety working-group → AISI engagement.** Already in
   progress per `docs/NIST-ENGAGEMENT-PLAN.md`. NIST itself isn't a
   buyer, but its endorsement opens doors at NIST-aligned agencies.

3. **AWS grant approval → AWS sales-channel referral.** Approved Vercel
   / AWS open-source grant recipients sometimes receive referral
   introductions to enterprise customers. Ask explicitly upon approval.

4. **Fintech CISO → friendly first logo.** Mid-market fintech CISOs
   (50–500 person companies, payments-adjacent) move 4× faster than
   government. Trade "$25K pilot fee" for "free 30-day shadow + logo
   rights." A logo at month 4 unblocks every government conversation.

5. **Y Combinator alumni network / similar founder communities.** If you
   have access, ask explicitly for fraud-control-buyer warm intros.

Cold government outreach should run in parallel with these — but treat
them as the primary acceleration path, not the cold sequence.

## Stop-loss criteria

If at day 60:
- Zero replies across 50+ sends → email channel is dead, change channel
  (LinkedIn DMs to procurement officers, conference outreach, paid
  webinar) or change segment (mid-market fintech vs government).
- Zero scheduled first calls → the pitch isn't landing, rewrite it.
- AWS grant denied → trigger Plan B (`PLAN-B-IF-AWS-DENIED.md`).

If at day 90:
- Zero late-stage deals → re-evaluate whether to continue the founder-
  led sales motion or hire a part-time fractional sales contact (~$5K/mo
  with commission) before another 90-day cycle.

## What success at 90 days unlocks

1 active late-stage deal + 1 FY27 budget slot + 3–5 warm conversations
is enough to:
- Justify $30K–$50K of pre-revenue burn through Q4 2026 from runway
- Stand up a "design partner" page with 1+ named institutional logo
- Make AWS / AAIF announcements to recruit additional pilots
- Avoid the trap of "we're 6 months in, where's revenue?"

---

## What success at 90 days does NOT unlock

- Series A. You need 2+ paid customers before any institutional VC
  takes a regulated-industry-trust-protocol seriously.
- Hiring. Don't hire a salesperson, marketer, or engineer until paid
  revenue covers 6 months of their loaded cost.
- Multi-vertical expansion (FinGuard / AgentGuard). Stay on GovGuard
  until 1 production pilot is operational and renewing.
