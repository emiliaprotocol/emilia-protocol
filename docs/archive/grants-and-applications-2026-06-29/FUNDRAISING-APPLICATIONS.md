## Y Combinator  (ycombinator.com/apply)

**Company URL:** https://emiliaprotocol.ai

**Describe what you do in 50 characters or less:**
> Proof a human approved an AI agent's action.

**What is your company going to make?**
> EMILIA is an open standard and toolkit for *authorization receipts*: cryptographic, offline-verifiable proof that a named human approved an exact, irreversible AI-agent action before it executed — checkable by anyone, without trusting the operator. As agents move from answering to acting (moving money, changing records, deploying code), EMILIA is the layer that proves a specific human authorized the specific action. We monetize a managed product (GovGuard for government, FinGuard for finance, enterprise compliance) on top of the free, open protocol.

**Why did you pick this idea? Domain expertise? How do you know people need it?**
> I authored the IETF Internet-Draft and built the formally-verified protocol and verifiers solo. The need is concrete and regulatory: EU AI Act Article 14, NIST AI RMF, and the 2026 Five Eyes guidance all require human oversight of high-risk AI — and none of them ships a verifiable primitive. The strongest evidence it's real: two other groups (PSEA, DRP) independently converged on the same construction, and we're now co-authoring a survey to the IETF.

**Who are your competitors? What do you understand that they don't?**
> A cluster builds "agent receipts" (nobulex, Agent Receipts, ScopeBlind) and decision-time systems (OAuth, AuthZEN, OPA, Cerbos). What they miss: a *named human* signing the *exact* action on their own device, verifiable *offline* without trusting the operator, with separation of duties — and that the winner owns the open **standard + conformance suite**, not a proprietary product. Evidence is the moat, not a dashboard.

**How will you make money? How much could you make?**
> Open protocol = distribution (free, un-lockable, adopted as the standard). Revenue = the managed layer: GovGuard/FinGuard SaaS, enterprise compliance packs, and defense/air-gap deployments. The market is every irreversible action an AI agent will ever take in regulated settings — government disbursements, financial transfers, healthcare, critical infrastructure.

**How far along are you?**
> Published IETF I-D (-01) + a 3-author cross-draft survey before the IETF secdispatch chairs; formally-verified core (26 TLA+ safety properties, 0 errors across 413,137 states); offline verifiers in JavaScript, Python, and Go on npm; an MCP server; device-bound Face ID signoff exercised end-to-end on real hardware; and a DIU Project Spectrum Strike entry submitted. Pre-revenue, no customers yet — next milestone is a 60-day government observe-mode pilot.

**How long have you worked on this? Founders?**
> Solo founder; ~3 months of focused build (started March 2026). Actively seeking a technical/GTM co-founder.

**Why now?**
> Agents crossed from answering to acting in 2026 (Visa/OpenAI agentic payments). Regulation is arriving (EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF, Five Eyes). And no one owns the verifiable-authorization primitive yet. The standard that wins is the one that's ready when the mandate lands.
